# Readability and Maintainability

- **Use Common Table Expressions (CTEs)** – the `WITH` clause breaks complex queries into logical, reusable parts, improving readability.
- **Consistent formatting and naming** – uppercase SQL keywords, lowercase snake_case for identifiers, and concise aliases.
- **Comment wisely** – add brief comments explaining business logic or complex sections. Use `--` for single‑line comments and `/* … */` for longer explanations.
- **Parentheses for clarity** – explicitly group conditions in `WHERE` clauses to avoid precedence surprises.
